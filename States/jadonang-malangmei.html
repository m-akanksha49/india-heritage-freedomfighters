Jadonang Malangmei

Jadonang Malangmei was a spiritual leader, social reformer, and freedom fighter from Manipur who led a movement against British rule and exploitation in the Naga Hills during the 1920s and 1930s. He is considered a pioneer of Naga nationalism and a forerunner of India's tribal resistance movement.

Biography

Born in 1905 in Kambiron village in the Tamenglong district of Manipur, Jadonang Malangmei belonged to the Rongmei Naga tribe. From a young age, he showed spiritual inclination and later declared himself a messenger of God, advocating for the spiritual and political independence of his people.

He founded the Heraka religious movement, aiming to revive traditional Naga faith and culture which he believed was being eroded by British missionaries and administration. Along with religious revival, Jadonang also strongly opposed the forced labor system, taxes, and colonial interference in tribal life.

As his influence grew, so did the fear of the British administration. He began organizing tribal warriors and preaching messages of resistance. His growing popularity and calls to overthrow the British rule led to his arrest in 1931. Despite a lack of evidence, he was executed publicly in Imphal on August 29, 1931, at the age of just 26.

Though his life was short, his message lived on through his disciple, Rani Gaidinliu, who carried forward his spiritual and political mission against colonial rule.

Resistance Against British Rule

Jadonang’s resistance was rooted in cultural pride, religious identity, and political independence. He viewed British rule not just as political oppression but also as an existential threat to the traditions and freedom of the Naga people.

His resistance strategies included:

  • Reviving the Heraka religion to unify and mobilize the tribes
  • Refusing to pay colonial taxes and perform forced labor
  • Preaching the end of British rule through spiritual prophecy
  • Secretly preparing for an armed uprising

Though he was captured before a full-scale revolt could occur, his ideas laid the foundation for future resistance movements and tribal identity in Northeast India.

Lessons to Learn from Jadonang

Spiritual and Cultural Pride

Jadonang showed that cultural preservation can be a powerful form of resistance. He reminded his people to take pride in their roots.

Leadership at a Young Age

Despite being only in his 20s, Jadonang led a mass movement, proving age is no barrier to leadership when purpose is strong.

Martyrdom for Justice

He chose death over submission, becoming a martyr for his people’s freedom and dignity.

Legacy of Inspiration

His ideas inspired future generations, including Rani Gaidinliu, showing that true revolutions live beyond one life.

Legacy

Heraka Movement

The Heraka movement that Jadonang founded continues to survive as a religious and cultural tradition among the Zeliangrong Nagas.

Inspiration to Rani Gaidinliu

His disciple, Rani Gaidinliu, carried forward the anti-British struggle and became a celebrated national freedom fighter.

Acknowledged in History

Jadonang is now widely recognized as one of the earliest martyrs from Northeast India who resisted colonialism with indigenous ideology.

Honored by Nation

Several institutions and memorials in Manipur honor his contributions. His story is part of school syllabi and commemorations in the Northeast.
