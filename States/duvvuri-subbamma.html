Duvvuri Subbamma

Duvvuri Subbamma was a fearless activist and one of the pioneering women in India's independence movement, especially active in the Andhra region.

"Let every woman awaken to her rights and fight injustice without fear."

Biography

Born in 1881 in Rajahmundry, Andhra Pradesh, Duvvuri Subbamma was a prominent freedom fighter and social reformer. She joined the Indian National Congress and took part in several civil disobedience movements. As a fearless orator and leader, she mobilized large groups of women to participate in the freedom struggle during the Salt Satyagraha and Quit India Movement.

Subbamma worked tirelessly to empower women and promote khadi. She was arrested multiple times for her participation in protests and played a major role in building nationalistic fervor among Telugu-speaking people.

Legacy

Duvvuri Subbamma's contribution to India's independence is remembered with reverence. She helped awaken political consciousness among women and inspired future generations of female leaders. In her honor, India Post released a commemorative stamp in 1999.
