Matmur Jamoh

Matmur Jamoh was a prominent tribal leader from Arunachal Pradesh who resisted British rule and is remembered for his bold stand against colonial forces during the Anglo-Abor Wars.

Born

1871

Rottung Village, East Siang, Arunachal Pradesh

Died

1912 (presumed martyrdom)

Known For

Leader in the Anglo-Abor War of 1911

Tribe

Adi Tribe

"Freedom is not given, it is taken — even by those in the farthest corners of India."

Biography

Matmur Jamoh was a brave warrior of the Adi tribe in the Siang region of Arunachal Pradesh. He emerged as a key figure during the Anglo-Abor War of 1911, leading local tribal forces in active resistance against British colonial expansion.

He famously led an ambush that killed British political officer Noel Williamson, triggering British retaliation in the form of a large-scale military expedition. Despite facing overwhelming odds, Jamoh's knowledge of terrain and community support prolonged resistance efforts.

Struggles & Resistance

  • Advocated for tribal autonomy and sovereignty against colonial administration.
  • Led attacks to protect cultural and land rights of the Adi people.
  • Facilitated strategic retreats and counterattacks during British advances.

Timeline

Honors

  • Matmur Jamoh Memorial constructed in East Siang District.
  • His name included in Arunachal Pradesh's official list of freedom fighters.
  • Annual events held in his memory on state-level platforms.

Cultural Impact

Matmur Jamoh's acts of resistance are immortalized in oral folklore, traditional songs, and tribal storytelling. He remains a symbol of defiance, valor, and regional pride, especially among the Adi people. His story also strengthens the demand for greater recognition of Northeast India's role in India's freedom movement.

Legacy

Educational Influence

Schools and institutions use his name to inspire future generations.

Infrastructure

Streets and memorials named in his honor across Arunachal Pradesh.

Patriotic Symbol

Matmur Jamoh is celebrated as a symbol of tribal unity and resistance.
