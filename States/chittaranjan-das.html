Biography

Chittaranjan Das was born on November 5, 1870 in Calcutta to a prominent Bengali family. After completing his education in England where he became a Barrister, he returned to India and established himself as one of Calcutta's most successful lawyers. His dramatic defense of Sri Aurobindo in the Alipore Bomb Case (1908) brought him national fame.

Das gradually moved from law to politics, becoming a leading figure in the Indian National Congress. He played a crucial role in the Non-Cooperation Movement (1920-22) alongside Gandhi, but later formed the Swaraj Party in 1923 to continue constitutional agitation within the legislatures when the movement was called off.

As Mayor of Calcutta, he introduced significant municipal reforms. His vision of decentralized governance and empowerment at local levels was ahead of its time. Despite his aristocratic background, he gave up all his wealth and luxury to serve the nation, earning him the title "Deshbandhu". His untimely death in 1925 at age 54 was mourned by the entire nation.

Key Contributions to Freedom Struggle

  • Legal Defense: Saved many freedom fighters through brilliant legal arguments
  • Mass Mobilization: Brought educated middle class into freedom movement
  • Political Strategy: Founded Swaraj Party for legislative resistance
  • Urban Leadership: Connected nationalist movement with urban centers
  • Constitutional Approach: Advocated working within system when needed
  • Mentorship: Guided young leaders like Subhas Chandra Bose

Political Vision

Chittaranjan Das articulated a distinct political vision for India's freedom and governance:

  • Advocated for complete Swaraj (self-rule)
  • Proposed decentralized governance with strong local bodies
  • Envisioned Hindu-Muslim unity as foundation of nationalism
  • Supported constructive work alongside political agitation
  • Balanced radical aims with pragmatic methods
  • Foresaw importance of urban centers in national movement
