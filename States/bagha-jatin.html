Biography

Jatindranath Mukherjee, popularly known as Bagha Jatin (Tiger Jatin), was born on December 7, 1879 in Kushtia (now in Bangladesh). He earned his nickname "Bagha" (Tiger) after single-handedly fighting and killing a Royal Bengal tiger that attacked him.

As a revolutionary leader, he reorganized the Yugantar party and established a network of revolutionary cells across Bengal. His plan to import German arms during World War I to start a nationwide armed rebellion against the British was one of the most ambitious revolutionary plots of the independence movement.

His final confrontation with British police occurred at Balasore where, though severely outnumbered, he and his companions fought a heroic last stand on September 9, 1915. Severely wounded, he died the next day, becoming a legend in the revolutionary movement.
