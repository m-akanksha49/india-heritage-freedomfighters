Tanguturi Prakasam Pantulu

Renowned as "Andhra Kesari" (The Lion of Andhra), he was a fearless leader, lawyer, journalist, and the first Chief Minister of Andhra State.

Born

August 23, 1872

Vinodarayunipalem, Andhra Pradesh, India

Died

May 20, 1957 (aged 84)

Chennai, India

Roles

Lawyer, Freedom Fighter, Politician, Journalist

Title

"Andhra Kesari" (Lion of Andhra)

"I will bare my chest to bullets, but will never submit to tyranny."

Biography

Tanguturi Prakasam Pantulu was born in 1872 in a small village in Andhra Pradesh. He initially worked as a teacher and then studied law in England, becoming a successful lawyer. Inspired by the national movement, he gave up his legal career and joined the freedom struggle.

He became famous for his defiance of British rule, especially during the Simon Commission protests. As a journalist, he used his publication "Swarajya" to spread nationalist ideas. He was later elected the first Chief Minister of Andhra State in 1953.

Revolutionary Activities

1921

Left a successful legal practice to join the Non-Cooperation Movement.

1928

Led protests against the Simon Commission and famously bared his chest to British bullets.

1930s-40s

Active in Civil Disobedience and Quit India Movements; was imprisoned multiple times.

1953

Became the first Chief Minister of Andhra State after its formation.

Legacy

Political Icon

First Chief Minister of Andhra State, remembered for bold leadership and administrative reforms.

Institutions

Prakasam University and various institutions bear his name, recognizing his contribution to education and law.

Landmarks

Prakasam district and Prakasam Barrage stand as living tributes to his name and legacy.

Honors

Hailed as "Andhra Kesari" and commemorated through stamps, statues, and awards.
