Chaudhary Devi Lal

A patriot, peasant leader, and social reformer from Haryana, Devi Lal joined the freedom struggle at an early age and became a political icon for rural India.

Born

September 25, 1914

Teja Khera, Sirsa, Punjab (now Haryana)

Died

April 6, 2001 (aged 86)

New Delhi, India

Party

Lok Dal / Janata Dal

Known For

Freedom struggle, farmers' rights, former Deputy Prime Minister of India

"The strength of India lies in its villages and its farmers. Their voice must never be ignored."

Biography

Chaudhary Devi Lal was born into a family of farmers in Sirsa, Haryana. He became a part of the freedom struggle at just 16 years old by participating in protests against British rule. He was arrested and imprisoned multiple times, including during the Quit India Movement.

Post-independence, he became a champion of farmers and rural development. His legacy lives on as one of the most powerful voices for India's agrarian population.

Freedom Struggle & Contributions

1930

Joined the Civil Disobedience Movement and was arrested for participating in a protest rally.

1942

Took part in the Quit India Movement and imprisoned by the British.

1950s–70s

Organized farmer unions and fought for rural rights across Haryana and northern India.

Legacy

Champion of Farmers

Revered as “Tau” (elder), he empowered farmers and rural workers through activism and policy.

Deputy Prime Minister

Served as Deputy PM of India from 1989 to 1991 under the V.P. Singh government.

Devi Lal Institutions

Many universities and institutions across Haryana are named after him.

National Recognition

Commemorated with stamps, roads, and statues for his lifelong service to the nation.
