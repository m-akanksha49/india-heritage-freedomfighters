Indramani Badoni

Known as the "Gandhi of Uttarakhand," Indramani Badoni was a prominent leader in the movement for a separate Uttarakhand state and a champion of regional identity and environmental activism.

Born

December 24, 1924

Jakholi, Rudraprayag, Uttarakhand

Died

August 18, 1999

Dehradun, Uttarakhand

Known For

Uttarakhand statehood movement, social work, Gandhian activism

Legacy

Inspired peaceful protests, key figure in formation of Uttarakhand

"The voice of the hills must not be drowned in the plains."

Biography

Indramani Badoni emerged as a pivotal leader in the 1970s and 1990s Uttarakhand statehood movement. Rooted in Gandhian principles of non-violence and simplicity, he became the voice of the neglected hill regions of Uttar Pradesh. Badoni organized hunger strikes, peaceful marches, and raised cultural awareness about the region’s identity and rights.

His humble lifestyle and moral authority gave momentum to the demand for a separate state, which was finally realized in 2000, shortly after his death.

Timeline of Contributions

1970s

Began advocating for separate hill state and preservation of regional culture.

1994

Led massive protests and hunger strikes for statehood at Delhi's Boat Club and in Uttarakhand.

1998

Inspired youth-led movements and dialogues with national leaders.

2000

His vision fulfilled with the creation of Uttarakhand state.

Legacy

Statehood Pioneer

Revered as the father of Uttarakhand for championing statehood with peace.

Gandhian Leader

Lived a life of simplicity and non-violence, deeply influencing the hill populace.

Cultural Icon

Promoted local languages, traditions, and festivals to preserve regional identity.
