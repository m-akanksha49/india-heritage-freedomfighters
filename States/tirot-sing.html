Biography

Tirot Sing, also known as U Tirot Sing Syiem, was born in 1802 in Nongkhlaw, a Khasi kingdom in present-day Meghalaya. He became the Syiem (chief) of Nongkhlaw in 1826 after his uncle's death.

When British East India Company officials, led by David Scott, sought to build a road through Khasi territory connecting Bengal with Assam, Tirot Sing initially agreed. However, when he realized the British intended permanent occupation, he organized resistance in 1829.

For four years (1829-1833), Tirot Sing led guerrilla warfare against the British from the dense forests of the Khasi Hills. Using traditional weapons and knowledge of the terrain, his forces inflicted heavy casualties on the better-armed British troops. He was eventually captured in 1833 through betrayal and died in captivity in 1835.
