Kanneganti Hanumanthu (1870-1922) - The Lion of Palnadu

Kanneganti Hanumanthu

The fearless tribal leader from Andhra Pradesh who led the historic rebellion against British forest laws in the Palnadu region. His martyrdom in 1922 became a rallying point for tribal rights and anti-colonial resistance in South India.

Early Life and Background

Born in the small village of Minchalapadu in the Palnadu region, Hanumanthu belonged to the Golla (shepherd) community. From an early age, he witnessed how British forest laws:

  • Restricted traditional tribal access to forests
  • Imposed heavy taxes on forest produce collection
  • Criminalized age-old practices of the tribal communities

Growing up in this oppressive environment shaped his revolutionary consciousness and commitment to tribal rights.

The Forest Satyagraha (1921-1922)

1921

Beginning of Resistance

Hanumanthu organized villagers to refuse payment of the oppressive "Kattu Panchayati" (forest tax). He argued that the British had no right to tax what nature provided freely.

January 1922

Mass Movement

Over 5,000 villagers joined his protest. They collectively harvested forest produce without paying taxes, directly challenging British authority.

February 22, 1922

Martyrdom

British forces surrounded his village. After refusing to surrender, Hanumanthu was shot dead, becoming one of the earliest martyrs of tribal resistance in South India.

Impact of the Movement

Though brutally suppressed, Hanumanthu's rebellion:

  • Inspired similar forest satyagrahas across Madras Presidency
  • Forced the British to moderate some forest laws
  • Became a symbol of tribal resistance that continues to inspire modern movements

Ideology and Philosophy

Self-Rule

Believed in complete autonomy over local resources and rejection of colonial interference in traditional practices.

Direct Action

Advocated non-compliance with unjust laws through mass civil disobedience.

Community Rights

Fought for collective tribal rights over individual concessions from the British.

Historical Context

Hanumanthu's rebellion occurred during a critical period:

  • Following the Non-Cooperation Movement (1920)
  • Preceding the Civil Disobedience Movement (1930)
  • Amidst widespread tribal unrest across India against forest laws

His movement demonstrated how the freedom struggle wasn't limited to urban areas but had deep roots in rural and tribal India.

Hanumanthu's life teaches us the importance of:

  • Standing against unjust policies

  • Sacrifice for larger causes

His legacy continues to inspire environmental and tribal rights movements across India.
