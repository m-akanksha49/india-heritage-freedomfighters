Thakur Kushal Singh

A brave leader from Rajasthan who played a pivotal role during the Revolt of 1857 against British colonial rule, inspiring many with his courage and determination.

Born

1818

Auwa, Marwar, Rajasthan

Died

1873

Udaipur, Rajasthan

Known For

Leading the rebellion in Marwar during the 1857 revolt

Title

Thakur of Auwa

"Victory is not born from weapons alone, but from the will to be free."

Biography

Thakur Kushal Singh of Auwa was one of the most prominent leaders of Rajasthan during the First War of Independence in 1857. He organized and led a formidable resistance against the British forces in the region of Marwar.

As the Thakur of Auwa, he rallied local leaders and soldiers to resist colonial dominance, establishing Auwa as a significant center of resistance. His defiance and bravery inspired other princely states to reconsider their support of British rule.

Major Contributions

1857

Declared independence from British control and fortified the town of Auwa as a center of rebellion.

1857

Defeated British forces in several early skirmishes, including battles involving British commanders.

1858

Despite valiant resistance, the British captured Auwa. Kushal Singh escaped to Mewar and was granted refuge by the Maharana of Udaipur.

Legacy

Statue in Auwa

A statue commemorating Kushal Singh stands in Auwa to honor his leadership during the revolt.

Historical Recognition

Recognized in Rajasthan’s history as a fearless leader who resisted British imperialism at great personal cost.

Inspiration

Kushal Singh's leadership continues to inspire local pride and has become a symbol of Marwar's resistance.
