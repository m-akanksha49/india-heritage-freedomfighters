Sunderlal Bahuguna

Renowned environmentalist and social reformer from Uttarakhand, Sunderlal Bahuguna led several non-violent movements to protect nature and raise awareness about sustainable living and forest conservation.

Born

January 9, 1927

Maroda, Tehri Garhwal, Uttarakhand

Died

May 21, 2021

Rishikesh, Uttarakhand

Known For

Chipko Movement, Anti-Tehri Dam protests, Environmental activism

Legacy

Padma Vibhushan recipient, Voice of Himalayan ecology

"Ecology is the permanent economy."

Biography

Sunderlal Bahuguna was a pioneer of the Chipko Movement, which mobilized people to hug trees to prevent them from being cut down. Born in a remote village in Tehri Garhwal, he devoted his life to Gandhian principles of non-violence and sustainability.

He worked tirelessly to raise awareness about the importance of forest conservation, the impact of large dams, and the rights of indigenous people. He traveled across India to spread the message of ecological protection.

Timeline of Contributions

1973

Led the Chipko Movement in the Garhwal Himalayas, inspiring a generation of environmentalists.

1981

Launched a 5,000 km trans-Himalayan foot march to raise awareness about deforestation.

1990s

Protested against the Tehri Dam project, emphasizing the ecological dangers and displacement.

2009

Received the Padma Vibhushan, India's second-highest civilian award, for his environmental work.

Legacy

Chipko Movement

Inspired one of India’s most iconic grassroots environmental movements focused on tree conservation.

Ecological Reformer

Advocated sustainable development and ecological balance across India.

Awards

Honored with Padma Vibhushan and numerous international environmental recognitions.
