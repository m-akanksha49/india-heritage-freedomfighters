Sheel Bhadra Yajee

Indian Freedom Fighter and Revolutionary from Bihar

Sheel Bhadra Yajee

A dedicated nationalist who played a crucial role in India’s independence movement. He was closely associated with both Subhas Chandra Bose and Mahatma Gandhi, bridging revolutionary zeal with Gandhian principles.

"Sacrifice is the foundation of freedom."

Biography

Born in 1906 in Bihar, Sheel Bhadra Yajee was an active participant in India's freedom struggle. He was imprisoned several times by the British for his nationalist activities. He supported Subhas Chandra Bose’s Indian National Army (INA) but later aligned with Gandhian ideals. Yajee worked tirelessly for social reform, especially focusing on labor rights and anti-colonial movements in Bihar.

Timeline

1906: Born in Bhagalpur, Bihar.
1930s: Joined Congress Socialist Party; imprisoned during Salt Satyagraha.
1940s: Supported INA; closely worked with Subhas Chandra Bose.
Post 1947: Became member of Constituent Assembly of India.
1996: Passed away, remembered as a socialist and patriot.

Legacy

  • Indian postal department issued a commemorative stamp in his honor (2001).
  • Remembered for advocating socialism, nationalism, and labor rights.
  • Schools and roads in Bihar are named after him.
