Gopabandhu Das

Gopabandhu Das, fondly called "Utkalamani" (Gem of Odisha), was a freedom fighter, reformer, journalist, and poet who dedicated his life to public service. He emphasized education, social equality, and national service over personal gain and wealth.

Biography

Gopabandhu Das was born in Suando village in Odisha. After completing his early education, he pursued law in Calcutta. Despite becoming a successful lawyer, he gave up his profession to serve the people of Odisha and India. Moved by the struggles of the common man, he dedicated his life to education, social reform, and the freedom movement.

He founded the *Satyabadi Bana Vidyalaya* at Sakhigopal, a pioneering effort in nationalist education. Gopabandhu also started the newspaper *The Samaja* to promote awareness and patriotism among the people. As a member of the Indian National Congress, he participated in multiple sessions and campaigns.

His compassion extended beyond politics — during the 1918 flood in Odisha, he personally led rescue and relief missions. He even ignored the death of his own son to continue serving flood victims, demonstrating supreme sacrifice and public spirit.

Resistance and Reform

Although not known for armed resistance, Gopabandhu Das resisted British rule through constructive work. He promoted Swadeshi ideals, supported Congress initiatives, and fought against caste discrimination and educational inequality.

He believed that freedom would be meaningful only if people were socially and intellectually empowered. His newspaper *The Samaja* played a significant role in awakening nationalist sentiment in Odisha.

  • Advocated for education rooted in Indian values
  • Opposed untouchability and caste-based oppression
  • Used writing and journalism to fight ignorance and injustice
  • Promoted unity among Odias to assert linguistic and regional identity

Lessons to Learn from Gopabandhu Das

Selfless Service

He sacrificed personal comforts, including family duties, for the welfare of society.

Value of Education

Believed education should not just be academic but also build character and nationhood.

Empowering Through Journalism

Used media to educate, inspire, and mobilize people towards a common cause.

Humanitarianism

Risked his life during floods and epidemics to help others, displaying courage and compassion.

Unity and Regional Pride

Stressed the importance of unity among Odias and preserving their cultural and linguistic identity.

Legacy

Educational Pioneer

His school at Sakhigopal became a model of nationalist education combining Indian tradition and modernity.

The Samaja

*The Samaja*, started by him, remains one of Odisha’s most prominent newspapers even today.

Utkal Sammilani Contributor

Instrumental in the Odisha unification movement, advocating for the formation of Odisha as a separate linguistic state.

Remembered as Utkalamani

Odisha reveres him as ‘Utkalamani’ and his contributions are honored in schools, streets, and public memory.
