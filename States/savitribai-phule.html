Savitribai Phule

India's first female teacher, a radical feminist, and a pioneering social reformer who fought for women's education and against caste discrimination.

Born

January 3, 1831

Naigaon, Maharashtra

Died

March 10, 1897 (aged 66)

Pune, British India

Organizations

Satya Shodhak Samaj, Mahila Seva Mandal

Known For

First female teacher of India, women's education, anti-caste activism

"Awake, arise and educate. Smash traditions - liberate!"

Biography

Savitribai Phule was a revolutionary social reformer who dedicated her life to educating women and fighting against caste and gender oppression in 19th century India. Married at age 9 to Jyotirao Phule, she became India's first female teacher when she started teaching girls at the school they founded in Pune in 1848, despite facing violent opposition from orthodox society.

Along with her husband, she established 18 schools for girls and lower caste children, challenging the Brahminical orthodoxy that denied education to these groups. She also founded a care center for pregnant rape victims called "Balhatya Pratibandhak Griha" and fought against child marriage and sati.

Revolutionary Activities

1848

Started India's first school for girls in Pune at Bhide Wada, becoming India's first female teacher.

1852

Opened a school for lower caste girls, facing severe social opposition including having dung thrown at her.

1854

Published her first collection of poems, "Kavya Phule", becoming one of India's first modern women poets.

1873

Co-founded Satya Shodhak Samaj (Society of Truth Seekers) with Jyotirao Phule to fight caste discrimination.

1897

Died while caring for plague patients, cementing her legacy as a selfless social worker.

Legacy

Education Pioneer

Paved the way for women's education in India by starting the first school for girls and training other women teachers.

Feminist Icon

Challenged patriarchal norms by advocating for widow remarriage, women's rights, and against child marriage.

Anti-Caste Activism

Worked tirelessly to eradicate untouchability and caste discrimination through education and social reform.

Literary Contributions

Wrote powerful poetry and prose advocating social reform and women's empowerment in Marathi.

Life Lessons from Savitribai Phule

  • Courage in Adversity: Savitribai faced stones and dung being thrown at her for teaching girls, yet persisted. She teaches us to stand firm for what's right despite opposition.
  • Education as Liberation: Her belief that education could liberate women and lower castes shows how knowledge can break social shackles.
  • Intersectional Activism: She fought both caste and gender oppression simultaneously, teaching us that social justice must be inclusive.
  • Practical Compassion: By establishing shelters for widows and rape victims, she showed that real change requires both activism and direct service.
  • Partnership for Change: Her collaboration with Jyotirao Phule demonstrates how allies can work together to challenge oppressive systems.
  • Selfless Service: Dying while caring for plague patients, she embodied the ultimate sacrifice for humanity.
