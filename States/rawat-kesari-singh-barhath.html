Rawat Kesari Singh Barhath

A revolutionary from Rajasthan known for igniting patriotic sentiments among Indian rulers and actively resisting British imperialism.

Born

21 November 1872

Devpura, Shahpura, Rajasthan

Died

14 August 1941

Udaipur, Rajasthan

Known For

Encouraging nationalist revolts, Literary resistance

Family

Father of Kunwar Pratap Singh Barhath

"The sword of patriotism should be sharpened with the whetstone of sacrifice."

Biography

Kesari Singh Barhath was a Rajput nobleman turned revolutionary who used his influence and literary skills to inspire resistance against the British Raj. He was highly educated and skilled in Sanskrit, Hindi, and Rajasthani literature.

He was closely associated with nationalists and provided guidance to revolutionaries, including his own son and nephew, who played crucial roles in key revolutionary plots.

Freedom Movement Timeline

1907

Sent his famous poem to Maharana of Mewar urging him not to attend the Delhi Durbar.

1912

Inspired his son and nephew to participate in the Hardinge Bomb Plot.

1930s

Continued writing and guiding revolutionary youth in Rajasthan.

Legacy

Literary Patriot

Wrote poems and letters that sparked national pride among rulers and youth.

Revolutionary Family

Father of martyr Kunwar Pratap Singh Barhath, a notable figure in the freedom struggle.

Honored Legacy

Commemorated in Rajasthan for his bold voice against colonial submission.
