Vinayak Damodar Savarkar

Popularly known as Veer Savarkar, he was a revolutionary, poet, historian, and political leader who inspired radical change and self-rule in India.

Born

May 28, 1883

Bhagur, Maharashtra

Died

February 26, 1966 (aged 82)

Mumbai, India

Organizations

Abhinav Bharat, Hindu Mahasabha

Known For

Radical nationalism, Hindutva ideology, literary contributions

"One who believes that the nation is above everything else, even above himself."

Biography

Veer Savarkar was a fearless revolutionary, known for his uncompromising stance against British rule and his powerful writings. As a student in London, he organized resistance through Abhinav Bharat and wrote the first history of the 1857 revolt, portraying it as India's first war of independence.

He was arrested and sentenced to life imprisonment at the Cellular Jail in the Andamans, where he spent over a decade in harsh conditions. Even in prison, he wrote extensively and continued his call for independence and reform.

Revolutionary Activities

1904

Founded Abhinav Bharat Society to ignite armed revolution against British rule.

1909

Arrested in London for involvement in revolutionary activities and extradited to India.

1911

Sent to Cellular Jail in Andaman Islands; spent over 10 years in solitary confinement.

1937–1943

Led the Hindu Mahasabha and advocated for Hindu nationalism and social reform.

Legacy

Radical Revolutionary

Savarkar’s advocacy of armed struggle and direct confrontation made him a feared name among colonial authorities.

Literary Works

Author of "The First War of Indian Independence" and "Hindutva", among many powerful texts promoting nationalism.

Political Thought

Originator of the Hindutva ideology that still influences political thought in modern India.

Prison Legacy

His sufferings in the Andaman jail inspired generations to sacrifice for the motherland.
