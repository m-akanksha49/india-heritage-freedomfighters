Freedom Fighters of India

Remembering the heroes who fought for our independence

Potti Sreeramulu

A selfless freedom fighter and Gandhian whose 58-day fast unto death led to the formation of the first linguistic state of independent India - Andhra Pradesh. His supreme sacrifice earned him the title "Amarajeevi" (Immortal Being).

Born

March 16, 1901

Padamatapalli, Madras Presidency (present-day Andhra Pradesh)

Died

December 15, 1952 (aged 51)

Madras (now Chennai), India

Movement

Indian Independence Movement, Andhra State Movement

Legacy

Father of Andhra Pradesh, Linguistic Reorganization of States

"My ambition is to live as a servant and die as a servant of the people. If I die for the cause of Andhra, I shall be blessed."

Early Life and Education

Potti Sreeramulu was born into a middle-class family in Padamatapalli village in Nellore district (now part of Andhra Pradesh). His father, Guravayya, was a revenue inspector, and his mother, Mahalakshmamma, was a homemaker. From childhood, he showed remarkable compassion for the poor and downtrodden.

He completed his early education in Madras (now Chennai) and graduated with a Bachelor of Arts degree from the prestigious Madras Christian College. Despite coming from an affluent background, he chose to live simply and dedicated his life to public service.

Role in Indian Freedom Struggle

Inspired by Mahatma Gandhi's ideals, Sreeramulu joined the Indian National Congress in 1928. He actively participated in:

  • Salt Satyagraha (1930): Organized salt marches in Madras Presidency and was imprisoned for 18 months
  • Individual Satyagraha (1940): Among the first batch of satyagrahis selected by Gandhi
  • Quit India Movement (1942): Played key role in underground activities and was imprisoned for 3 years

His dedication was such that he donated all his property to the Harijan Welfare Fund and lived in Gandhian ashrams, following strict celibacy and simplicity.

The Andhra State Movement

After independence, Sreeramulu championed the cause of Telugu-speaking people who were dissatisfied being part of Madras State. The key events:

1951

Walked 1,400 miles across Andhra regions to gauge public opinion for separate state

Oct 19, 1952

Began fast-unto-death at Madras house of Bulusu Sambamurthy

Dec 15, 1952

Died after 58 days of fasting, creating massive public outcry

Oct 1, 1953

Andhra State formed - first linguistic state in independent India

The fast became a national event with daily bulletins in newspapers. Despite interventions by Nehru and Rajaji, Sreeramulu remained steadfast till his last breath.

Personal Life and Gandhian Values

Sreeramulu was a strict follower of Gandhian principles:

Lifestyle

  • Lived in huts and ashrams throughout his life
  • Wore only khadi and rejected all material comforts
  • Maintained celibacy and simple vegetarian diet

Social Work

  • Established schools for Harijan children
  • Worked extensively for temple entry movements
  • Promoted khadi and village industries

His selfless nature earned him the nickname "Amarajeevi" (Immortal Being) among common people who saw him as a living saint.

Legacy and Impact

State Formation

His sacrifice directly led to States Reorganization Act (1956) that redrew India's map along linguistic lines

Memorials

Over 50 major institutions including Potti Sreeramulu Telugu University, hospitals and roads named after him

Cultural Depictions

Featured in films like "Amarajeevi" (1986) and numerous literary works in Telugu

Commemorations

December 15 observed as "Amarajeevi Potti Sreeramulu Remembrance Day" in Andhra and Telangana

Lasting Influence

Sreeramulu's sacrifice established the principle that India's federal structure should respect linguistic identities. The Andhra movement became a template for subsequent statehood demands across India. His life continues to inspire political activists and social workers.
