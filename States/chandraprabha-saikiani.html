Chandraprabha Saikiani

A fearless reformer, writer, and freedom fighter who led social and political reform movements for women's rights in Assam. Her lifelong struggle against patriarchal norms and caste discrimination made her one of the most influential women in Assam's history.

Born

March 16, 1901

Daisagaon, Assam

Died

March 16, 1972 (aged 71)

Guwahati, Assam

Role

Social Reformer, Educationist, Writer, Freedom Fighter

Legacy

First woman political activist of Assam, founder of Asom Pradeshik Mahila Samiti

"Freedom must begin from the minds of our daughters and mothers. Until women are free, India cannot be truly free."

Early Life and Education

Chandraprabha Saikiani was born on March 16, 1901, in a Kaibarta (fishermen) family in Daisigaon village of Kamrup district, Assam. Despite belonging to a marginalized community and facing caste discrimination, she displayed exceptional academic talent from a young age.

Her father, Ritaram Mazumdar, recognized her potential and supported her education, which was unusual for girls at that time. She completed her early education at the Sonaram Baruah School in Guwahati, becoming one of the first girls from her community to receive formal education.

In 1918, she joined Cotton College in Guwahati, where she faced discrimination for being from a lower caste. Undeterred, she continued her studies and became involved in social reform activities while still a student.

Major Contributions

Women's Rights Activism

Founded the Asom Pradeshik Mahila Samiti in 1926, which became Assam's first women's organization. Organized protests against purdah system, child marriage, and polygamy. Campaigned for widow remarriage and women's education.

Educational Reforms

Established schools for girls in rural Assam. Advocated for co-education and fought against caste-based discrimination in educational institutions. Served as headmistress of several schools throughout her career.

Freedom Movement

Active participant in Non-Cooperation Movement (1920-22). Organized women's participation in Civil Disobedience Movement. Spread Gandhian ideals across Assam and mobilized women for the freedom struggle.

Literary Contributions

Authored several books including her autobiography "Mor Jivan Sowaran". Wrote extensively on women's issues and social reform. Her works became important documents of Assam's social history.

Social Reforms

Led the historic "Harijan" temple entry movement in 1925, fighting against caste discrimination. Campaigned against the dowry system and worked to improve conditions for widows and destitute women.

Political Participation

One of the first women to actively participate in Assam's political life. Served in various government committees post-independence to advise on women's welfare and education policies.

Literary Works

Chandraprabha Saikiani was not just a social reformer but also a prolific writer who used literature as a tool for social change. Her works reflected her experiences and the struggles of women in Assam:

  • Mor Jivan Sowaran (My Life's Journey) - Her autobiography documenting her struggles and achievements
  • Pitribhumi - A collection of essays on social issues
  • Seuji Pator Kahini - Stories highlighting women's issues
  • Numerous articles in Assamese journals advocating women's rights and social reform

Recognition and Awards

  • Padma Shri (1959) - One of India's highest civilian honors for her contributions to social work
  • Posthumously awarded the Assam Sahitya Sabha's Sadhani Award
  • The Assam government has instituted the Chandraprabha Saikiani Award for women social workers
  • Several educational institutions and women's hostels named after her across Assam
  • In 2002, India Post issued a commemorative stamp in her honor

Legacy and Impact

Educational Legacy

Paved the way for girls' education in Assam. Several schools, colleges and hostels named after her continue to inspire new generations of students.

Women's Movement

Her organization Asom Pradeshik Mahila Samiti remains active. She inspired subsequent waves of feminist activism in Northeast India.

Literary Influence

Her writings are studied as important social documents. Inspired generations of Assamese women writers to address social issues.

Chandraprabha Saikiani's life demonstrates how one determined individual can challenge deep-rooted social prejudices. Her courage in fighting both British colonialism and social oppression makes her a unique figure in India's freedom struggle. Today, she remains a symbol of resistance and empowerment for marginalized communities, especially women in Assam.
