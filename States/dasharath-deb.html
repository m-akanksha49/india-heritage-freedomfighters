Dasharath Deb

A freedom fighter and socialist leader, Dasharath Deb led Tripura's integration into India, became the first Chief Minister (1978), and championed tribal rights, education, and state development.

Biography

Born into a modest Tripuri family, Dasharath Deb took part in pre-independence and post-merger politics. Imprisoned during the 1940s for anti-colonial activities, he later became instrumental in the democratic and socialist governance of Tripura.

As Chief Minister in 1978, he sought peace in insurgency-hit regions, backed tribal empowerment, and advanced education and health across multiple communities.
