Shivaram Rajguru

Shivaram Rajguru was a revolutionary freedom fighter who played a key role in India's struggle for independence alongside Bhagat Singh and Sukhdev.

Born

August 24, 1908

Khed, Maharashtra, British India

Died

March 23, 1931 (aged 22)

Lahore, Punjab, British India

Organizations

Hindustan Socialist Republican Association (HSRA)

Known For

Revolutionary activities, assassination of British officer John Saunders

"If the deaf are to hear, the sound has to be very loud."

Biography

Shivaram Hari Rajguru was born in Khed, Maharashtra, which was later renamed Rajgurunagar in his honor. From a young age, he was inspired by the growing nationalist movement and revolutionary leaders who were fighting British colonial rule.

He joined the Hindustan Socialist Republican Association (HSRA) and was known for his fearlessness, patriotism, and intense belief in India's freedom. His alliance with Bhagat Singh and Sukhdev led him to participate in one of the most iconic acts of resistance against the British.

Revolutionary Activities

1928

Joined HSRA and became active in revolutionary planning and missions across northern India.

1928

Participated in the assassination of British officer John Saunders to avenge the death of Lala Lajpat Rai during a protest against the Simon Commission.

1931

Executed along with Bhagat Singh and Sukhdev on March 23, 1931, in Lahore Central Jail. His martyrdom inspired countless others to join the freedom struggle.

Legacy

Memorials

Rajgurunagar (formerly Khed) is named after him. Statues and memorials across India honor his sacrifice.

Cultural Impact

Rajguru has been portrayed in many films and books, particularly those focusing on the trio of Bhagat Singh, Rajguru, and Sukhdev.

Youth Icon

He remains an inspiration for youth, symbolizing courage and patriotism at a young age.

Shaheed Diwas

March 23 is commemorated as Shaheed Diwas to honor Rajguru, Bhagat Singh, and Sukhdev's sacrifice.
