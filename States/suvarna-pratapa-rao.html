Suravaram Pratapa Reddy

Known as **“Telangana Vythalikudu” (Torch‑Bearer)**, he was a pioneering historian, journalist, social reformer, and freedom fighter under the Nizam's Hyderabad, championing Telugu culture, education, and Dalit rights.

Born

May 28, 1896

Boravelli (now Mahabubnagar district), Hyderabad State

Died

1953

Hyderabad, India

Professions

Historian, Journalist, Social Reformer, Lawyer

Affiliation

Andhra Mahasabha, Library Movement

"Without our language, our identity is erased—Telugu is our soul."

Biography

Born in 1896 near Gadwal, Pratapa Reddy earned his BA and BL degrees from Presidency College, Madras. His early career as a lawyer didn’t deter his passion for Telugu culture. In 1926, he founded the influential newspaper Golconda Patrika, boldly challenging Nizam's Urdu deep control over Hyderabad :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}.

A fierce advocate for social reform, he organized inter-caste feasts, encouraged libraries in rural Telangana, and fought caste discrimination in the 1930s :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}. As president of the Andhra Mahasabha in 1930, he united diverse communities under the Telugu identity :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}.

Lessons to Learn

Cultural Revival

He rebuilt Telugu identity during oppression, showing cultural assertion as resistance.

Unity in Diversity

Promoted inclusion across caste, enriching Telangana's social fabric.

Education as Empowerment

Empowered people through libraries and literary awareness.

Fearless Journalism

Used the pen to counter tyranny under colonial-like authoritarianism.
