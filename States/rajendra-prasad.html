Dr. Rajendra Prasad

India's first President, eminent freedom fighter, and one of the chief architects of the Indian Constitution - a man who dedicated his life to the service of the nation.

Born

December 3, 1884

Ziradei, Bihar, British India

Died

February 28, 1963 (aged 78)

Patna, Bihar, India

Title

First President of India

Known For

Indian independence movement, Constitution framing, Nation-building

"The constitution is a sacred document that lays down the path for our nation's progress."

Biography

Born on December 3, 1884 in Ziradei village of Bihar, Rajendra Prasad showed exceptional academic brilliance from childhood. He completed his Masters in Law from Calcutta University with first division, topping the university. While practicing law in Calcutta, he came under the influence of Mahatma Gandhi during the Champaran Satyagraha (1917) and abandoned his lucrative legal career to join India's freedom struggle.

Dr. Prasad played a pivotal role in the Non-Cooperation Movement (1920), Salt Satyagraha (1930), and Quit India Movement (1942), enduring several imprisonments. As President of the Constituent Assembly (1946-1950), he guided the framing of India's Constitution with wisdom and foresight. Elected as independent India's first President in 1950, he served two terms until 1962, setting high standards for the office.

Despite holding the highest constitutional position, he maintained a simple lifestyle, donating his salary to the Prime Minister's National Relief Fund. His profound scholarship (he mastered Persian, Hindi, Sanskrit, and English) and deep understanding of India's civilizational ethos made him uniquely qualified to help shape the new republic.

Key Contributions to Nation-Building

  • Constitution Making: Presided over 11 sessions of Constituent Assembly debates
  • Parliamentary Democracy: Established conventions for President's role in democratic system
  • Social Harmony: Worked to integrate princely states and promote communal harmony
  • Agricultural Reforms: Advocated for land reforms and farmers' welfare
  • Education: Emphasized universal education and established several educational institutions
  • Foreign Policy: Represented India in international forums during formative years

Lessons from Dr. Rajendra Prasad's Life

Integrity in Public Life

Dr. Prasad set unmatched standards of personal integrity, refusing special privileges and donating his salary, teaching us that public service demands sacrifice.

Lifelong Learning

His mastery of multiple languages and continuous intellectual growth shows the importance of constant learning for nation-building.

Consensus Building

As Constituent Assembly President, he harmonized diverse viewpoints, demonstrating that democracy thrives on accommodation.

Simplicity & Humility

Despite holding highest office, he maintained Gandhian simplicity, proving that true greatness lies in humility.

Constitutional Values

His steadfast commitment to constitutional principles established strong foundations for Indian democracy.

Literary Contributions

Dr. Prasad was a prolific writer whose works provide deep insights into India's freedom struggle and constitutional journey:

  • "India Divided" (1946): Analysis of Hindu-Muslim question
  • "Satyagraha at Champaran": First-hand account of Gandhi's first satyagraha
  • "Atmakatha" (Autobiography): His life story till 1946
  • "Bharatiya Shiksha": Vision for India's education system
  • "Since Independence": Reflections on early years of independence

Legacy

Educational Institutions

Several universities including Rajendra Agricultural University and Rajendra Law University honor his legacy.

Memorials

Rajendra Smriti Sangrahalaya in Patna preserves his personal artifacts and documents.

Awards & Honors

Bharat Ratna (1962), commemorative stamps, and his portrait in Parliament.

Constitutional Traditions

The conventions he established continue to guide India's presidency.
