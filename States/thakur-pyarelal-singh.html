Biography

Thakur Pyarelal Singh was born in 1891 in Rajasthan's Nagaur district. Belonging to a family of landowners, he shocked the establishment by becoming a champion of peasant rights. Inspired by Gandhi's ideals, he dedicated his life to improving the conditions of farmers and challenging the oppressive feudal system supported by the British.

In the 1920s, he emerged as a prominent leader of the peasant movement in Rajasthan, organizing farmers against excessive taxation and forced labor. His unique approach combined Gandhian non-violence with firm resistance against exploitation. He established several organizations to unite farmers across caste and community lines.

During the Quit India Movement of 1942, Thakur Pyarelal Singh played a crucial role in mobilizing rural Rajasthan against British rule. Post-independence, he continued working for land reforms and farmers' welfare until his death in 1959, leaving behind a legacy of peasant empowerment.
