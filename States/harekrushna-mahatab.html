Harekrushna Mahatab

Harekrushna Mahatab was a prominent freedom fighter, politician, writer, and historian from Odisha. Known as the "Lion of Odisha", he played a key role in the Indian independence movement and later became the first Chief Minister of Odisha. His contributions to social reform, education, and regional identity have left a lasting impact on the state and the nation.

Biography

Harekrushna Mahatab was born in 1899 in Agarpada village of Odisha. He was deeply influenced by the Indian National Movement and Mahatma Gandhi’s ideals. He left his education at Ravenshaw College to join the Non-Cooperation Movement in 1921. A committed satyagrahi, he was imprisoned multiple times for his role in the independence struggle.

He was an ardent advocate for Odisha's linguistic and cultural identity and played a key role in the formation of Odisha as a separate state in 1936. Post-independence, Mahatab served as Odisha’s first Chief Minister and worked on industrial development, education, and rehabilitation after the partition.

He was also an accomplished writer and historian. His literary works and editorials enriched Odia journalism and literature. Mahatab also served as the Union Minister of Commerce and Industry and later became the Governor of Bombay (now Mumbai) from 1955 to 1956.

Resistance and Political Leadership

Harekrushna Mahatab was at the forefront of India's freedom movement in Odisha. He was imprisoned during key movements like the Salt Satyagraha and Quit India Movement. Despite repeated arrests, he continued to campaign for civil rights, social upliftment, and political autonomy.

After independence, he was instrumental in establishing Odisha as a stable and progressive state. His governance was marked by industrial development, educational expansion, and infrastructure projects.

  • Strong advocate of Odisha's statehood based on language and culture
  • Key figure in the Indian National Congress in Eastern India
  • Promoted rural development and flood control in Odisha
  • Initiated reforms in higher education and administration

Lessons to Learn from Harekrushna Mahatab

Leadership with Vision

Mahatab believed in long-term development and worked toward a culturally strong and economically independent Odisha.

Power of Education

He prioritized education as a tool for social and national transformation.

Cultural Preservation

Promoted Odia language and literature, laying the foundation for a strong regional identity.

Service Over Position

Even as Chief Minister and Governor, he remained committed to grassroots service and welfare.

Journalistic Impact

As founder of the journal *Prajatantra*, he used journalism to voice people’s concerns and raise political awareness.
