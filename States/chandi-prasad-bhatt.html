Chandi Prasad Bhatt

A pioneering environmentalist and social activist from Uttarakhand, known for his integral role in the Chipko Movement and advocacy for rural communities.

Born

June 23, 1934

Gopeshwar, Uttarakhand

Known For

Chipko Movement, Environmental Activism

Awards

Padma Bhushan, Ramon Magsaysay Award

Legacy

Icon of grassroots environmentalism in India

"Ecology is the permanent economy."

Biography

Chandi Prasad Bhatt was born in a remote Himalayan village and was deeply influenced by the hardships faced by local villagers and the destruction of forests. In the 1960s, he founded the Dasholi Gram Swarajya Mandal (DGSM), a cooperative to provide employment to local people and protect the forest.

He played a central role in organizing the Chipko Movement in the 1970s, where villagers hugged trees to prevent deforestation. His activism highlighted the interdependence of people and forests and drew national attention to sustainable development.

Timeline of Contributions
