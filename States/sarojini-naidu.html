Sarojini Naidu

The Nightingale of India, a freedom fighter, poet, and the first woman Governor of an Indian state.

Born

February 13, 1879

Hyderabad, British India

Died

March 2, 1949 (aged 70)

Lucknow, India

Known As

Nightingale of India

Notable Roles

Poet, Politician, Freedom Fighter, Governor

"We want deeper sincerity of motive, a greater courage in speech and earnestness in action."

Biography

Sarojini Naidu was born on February 13, 1879, in Hyderabad to Aghorenath Chattopadhyay, a scientist and Barada Sundari Devi, a poetess. A child prodigy, she wrote her first poem at the age of 12 and earned recognition as the "Nightingale of India" for her beautiful poetry.

After studying in England, she returned to India and became involved in the Indian independence movement under the guidance of Gopal Krishna Gokhale and later Mahatma Gandhi. She was a prominent leader in the Civil Disobedience Movement and was imprisoned several times by the British authorities.

After independence, she became the first woman Governor of the United Provinces (now Uttar Pradesh), serving from 1947 until her death in 1949.

Key Contributions

1905

Joined the Indian independence movement after the partition of Bengal, becoming a prominent orator and leader.

1917

Founded the Women's Indian Association to fight for women's rights and suffrage.

1925

Became the first Indian woman president of the Indian National Congress.

1930

Played a leading role in the Salt Satyagraha and was imprisoned by the British.

1947

Became the first woman Governor of the United Provinces after independence.

Literary Works

Sarojini Naidu was a celebrated poet who wrote in English. Her poetry was known for its lyrical quality and Indian themes:

  • "The Golden Threshold" (1905) - Her first collection of poems
  • "The Bird of Time" (1912) - Containing her famous poem "In the Bazaars of Hyderabad"
  • "The Broken Wing" (1917) - Dedicated to "the cause of freedom"
  • "The Feather of the Dawn" (published posthumously in 1961)

Her poetry often explored themes of love, death, patriotism, and the beauty of Indian life and landscapes.

"A country's greatness lies in its undying ideals of love and sacrifice that inspire the mothers of the race."

Legacy

Memorials

Several institutions named after her including Sarojini Naidu College for Women, Sarojini Naidu Medical College, and her childhood home "Golden Threshold" which is now a university.

Literary Influence

Recognized as one of India's most important English-language poets who brought Indian themes to global attention.

Women's Rights

Pioneer of women's empowerment in India, inspiring generations of women to participate in public life.

National Women's Day

Her birthday (February 13) is celebrated as National Women's Day in India to honor her contributions.
