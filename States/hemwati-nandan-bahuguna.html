Hemwati Nandan Bahuguna

A notable political leader and freedom fighter from Uttarakhand, Hemwati Nandan Bahuguna was deeply involved in India's independence struggle and later contributed to the development of the region.

Biography

Hemwati Nandan Bahuguna was born in Pauri Garhwal and actively took part in the Quit India Movement during the 1940s. He was arrested and jailed by British authorities for his nationalist activities. Post-independence, he held several important political offices including Chief Minister of Uttar Pradesh and Union Cabinet Minister. He worked towards education and social reform, and is remembered for his commitment to democracy and accountability.
