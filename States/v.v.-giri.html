Varahagiri Venkata Giri (V.V. Giri)

Freedom fighter, trade union leader, statesman, and the fourth President of India. He dedicated his life to labor rights, democracy, and public service.

Biography

V.V. Giri was born into a Telugu-speaking Brahmin family in Berhampur (present-day Odisha). He pursued his education at Khallikote College and later at University College Dublin, Ireland, where he was influenced by nationalist ideas and came into contact with Irish revolutionaries.

He returned to India and joined the Indian National Congress. Giri actively participated in the Non-Cooperation Movement (1920) and was imprisoned multiple times for his involvement in the freedom struggle. He soon became deeply involved in the labor movement and trade union activism, establishing himself as a key voice for workers’ rights across the country.

He later served in several key roles: as a minister in the Madras Presidency, High Commissioner to Ceylon (Sri Lanka), Governor of Uttar Pradesh, Kerala, and Mysore, and finally, as Vice President and President of India.

Ideology and Contributions

  • Trade Union Leadership: Advocated for workers’ rights and organized labor unions nationwide. He laid the foundation for India's labor laws and fair wage policies.
  • Gandhian Principles: Despite his administrative roles, he remained committed to simplicity, social justice, and nationalism throughout his life.
  • Presidency: His presidency was marked by political independence, as he was the only elected President who won as an independent candidate.
  • Diplomacy & Statesmanship: As a seasoned governor and diplomat, he built bridges across diverse regions and helped maintain political stability.

Legacy

Champion of Labor

Revered as the father of India’s labor movement. V.V. Giri’s efforts shaped India’s labor policies post-independence.

Bharat Ratna

In 1975, he was awarded the Bharat Ratna for his contributions to politics, public service, and labor rights.

Commemorations

Institutions like the V.V. Giri National Labour Institute in Noida are named in his honor.

Global Outlook

As High Commissioner and statesman, he promoted India’s global presence and democratic values abroad.
