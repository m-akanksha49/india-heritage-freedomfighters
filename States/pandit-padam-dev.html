Pandit Padam Dev

The Gandhi of Himachal who led the Praja Mandal movement against princely rule.

Born

1893

Jubbal, Himachal Pradesh

Died

1967

Shimla, Himachal Pradesh

Movement

Praja Mandal Movement

Known As

Gandhi of Himachal

"The hills shall be free, and its people shall govern themselves."

Biography

Born in Jubbal state, Pandit Padam Dev became the foremost leader of the Praja Mandal movement that fought against the oppressive rule of hill princely states. Inspired by Gandhi, he organized peaceful protests and civil disobedience against the autocratic rulers.

He was imprisoned multiple times by the princely rulers but continued his struggle for democratic rights. After independence, he worked tirelessly for the merger of hill states into Himachal Pradesh.

Key Contributions

1939

Founded the Jubbal Praja Mandal to fight for people's rights

1942

Led Quit India Movement activities in hill states despite repression

1946

Organized massive protests against forced labor (begar) system

1948

Played key role in merger of princely states into Himachal
