Sachindra Lal Singh

The first democratically elected Chief Minister of Tripura (1963–1971) after its transition from princely rule. A stalwart in Tripura’s integration, his tenure focused on rehabilitation of refugees, tribal welfare, industrial and educational expansion.

Biography

Sachindra Lal Singh was a founding Congress MLA in the 1950s. He supported Tripura’s merger with India, guided refugee settlement from East Pakistan, and pushed to recognize Tripuri identity and land rights through legislation like the Tribal Claims Commission Acts (1960s).

Reforms & Administration

  • Sponsored tribal land and identity protections post-merger.

  • Encouraged small-scale industries and educational institutions.
  • Maintained communal harmony among Tripuri, Bengali, and other groups.
