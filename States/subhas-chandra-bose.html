Subhas Chandra Bose

The charismatic leader who challenged British imperialism and founded the Indian National Army to liberate India through armed resistance.

Born

January 23, 1897

Cuttack, Orissa Division, Bengal Presidency

Died

August 18, 1945 (aged 48)

Taihoku, Taiwan (disputed)

Organizations

Indian National Congress, Forward Bloc, Indian National Army

Known For

Founding INA, "Netaji" title, Revolutionary leadership

"Give me blood, and I shall give you freedom!"

Biography

Subhas Chandra Bose, popularly known as Netaji, was born on January 23, 1897, in Cuttack, Orissa, to Janak Nath Bose and Prabhavati Dutt Bose. He was the ninth child in a family of fourteen children. His father was a prominent lawyer and public prosecutor.

Bose was an exceptional student who topped the matriculation examination in 1913 and graduated with a First Class in Philosophy from Presidency College, Calcutta. He then went to England in 1919 to prepare for the Indian Civil Services examination, which he cleared with distinction, ranking fourth.

However, inspired by the nationalist movement and disgusted with British policies, Bose resigned from the ICS in 1921 and returned to India to join the freedom struggle under the mentorship of Chittaranjan Das.

Political Journey and Revolutionary Activities

1921

Resigned from Indian Civil Services to join the independence movement. Started working with Chittaranjan Das in Calcutta.

1924-1927

Imprisoned by British authorities for revolutionary activities. Used this time to study and write extensively.

1938-1939

Elected President of Indian National Congress twice. Resigned due to differences with Gandhi and other leaders over methods of struggle.

1939

Founded the Forward Bloc within the Congress to consolidate political left, but later made it a separate political party.

1941

Escaped from house arrest in Calcutta and traveled via Afghanistan to Germany, seeking international support for India's independence.

1943

Reached Southeast Asia and took command of the Indian National Army (Azad Hind Fauj) from Rash Behari Bose.

1944

Led the INA's advance into India through Burma. The famous battle cry "Chalo Delhi" was given during this campaign.

1945

Reportedly died in a plane crash in Taiwan on August 18, though the circumstances remain controversial and disputed.

The Indian National Army

One of Bose's most significant contributions to the independence movement was the formation and leadership of the Indian National Army (INA), also known as Azad Hind Fauj. The INA was composed of Indian prisoners of war captured by Japan during World War II and Indian expatriates in Southeast Asia.

Under Bose's leadership, the INA grew to about 43,000 troops and played a crucial role in the Burma Campaign. The army had its own government, the Provisional Government of Free India (Azad Hind), established in Singapore in 1943 with Bose as its head.

The INA's march towards India with the slogan "Chalo Delhi" (March to Delhi) inspired millions of Indians and demonstrated that Indians could organize and fight effectively against British rule.

"It is our duty to pay for our liberty with our own blood. The freedom that we shall win through our sacrifice and exertion, we shall be able to preserve with our own strength."

Ideology and Philosophy

Subhas Chandra Bose believed in complete independence and was convinced that only through armed resistance could India achieve freedom. His political philosophy included:

  • Total independence from British rule through any means necessary
  • Socialism and economic equality
  • Unity of all religions and communities
  • Strong national defense and military preparedness
  • International cooperation against fascism and imperialism
  • Women's empowerment and gender equality

Bose was also a progressive leader who established the Rani of Jhansi Regiment, the first women's unit in the INA, led by Captain Lakshmi Sahgal. This demonstrated his commitment to gender equality in the freedom struggle.

"One individual may die for an idea, but that idea will, after his death, incarnate itself in a thousand lives."

Legacy

National Recognition

His birthday, January 23, is celebrated as Parakram Diwas. Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose International Airport in Kolkata is named after him.

INA Trials Impact

The INA trials in Red Fort (1945-46) became a symbol of resistance and contributed significantly to India's independence movement.

Memorials

Numerous statues, institutions, and memorials across India including the INA Memorial at Red Fort and Netaji Bhawan in Kolkata.

Educational Legacy

Several universities and educational institutions bear his name, continuing his vision of education and national development.

Mysteries and Controversies

The circumstances of Subhas Chandra Bose's death remain one of the most debated topics in Indian history. While officially he is believed to have died in a plane crash in Taiwan on August 18, 1945, several theories and investigations have questioned this account:

  • Multiple government commissions have investigated his death with varying conclusions
  • Some theories suggest he survived and lived in disguise in India or elsewhere
  • Declassified files and testimonies have added to the mystery rather than resolving it
  • The debate continues to this day with regular demands for declassification of related documents
