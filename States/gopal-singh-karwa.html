Gopal Singh Kharwa

A courageous tribal leader from Mewar, Rajasthan, Gopal Singh Kharwa is remembered for his fearless resistance against British rule and advocacy for the rights of tribal people.

Born

1880

Mewar, Rajasthan

Died

Unknown

Rajasthan

Known For

Tribal Upliftment, Anti-British Movements

Community

Bhils of Mewar region

"We shall not bow to oppression — even if it costs our lives."

Biography

Gopal Singh Kharwa emerged as a strong voice of resistance from the Bhil tribal community in Mewar during the colonial era. Though lacking formal education, he led his people against oppressive British revenue practices and unjust treatment of tribal populations.

He became a symbol of defiance and leadership among the tribal groups, organizing small revolts and mobilizing people to resist British authority in forest regions and remote villages.

Resistance Activities Timeline

1905

Started organizing tribal groups in the Mewar region to resist forest laws and forced taxation.

1912

Led protests and direct action campaigns in tribal belts of southern Rajasthan.

1920s

Supported the Non-Cooperation Movement and spread nationalist ideas among tribal communities.

Legacy

Tribal Leader

One of the earliest tribal leaders to raise awareness of rights among the Bhils in Mewar.

Anti-Colonial Icon

Challenged British authority through direct action, inspiring tribal youth.

Cultural Inspiration

Featured in Rajasthan's oral traditions and folk songs as a symbol of bravery.
