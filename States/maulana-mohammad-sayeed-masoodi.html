Biography

Maulana Mohammad Sayeed Masoodi was born in 1905 in Anantnag, Jammu and Kashmir. He was a prominent Islamic scholar, freedom fighter, and political leader who played a crucial role in the Indian independence movement and the integration of Jammu & Kashmir with India.

Masoodi received his early education in Anantnag and later studied Islamic theology. He became actively involved in the freedom struggle during his youth and joined the National Conference under Sheikh Abdullah's leadership. Masoodi served as the General Secretary of the National Conference for many years and was a close associate of Sheikh Abdullah.

During the crucial period of 1947-48, Masoodi played a pivotal role in Kashmir's accession to India. He was instrumental in mobilizing public opinion in favor of joining India when Pakistani tribal raiders attacked Kashmir. Masoodi worked tirelessly to counter Pakistani propaganda and helped organize resistance against the invaders.

After independence, Masoodi continued to serve Jammu and Kashmir in various capacities, including as a member of the Constituent Assembly of Jammu and Kashmir. He remained committed to secular values and worked for the welfare of all communities in the state.

Role in Kashmir's Accession to India

Maulana Masoodi played a crucial role in Jammu and Kashmir's accession to India in 1947 when Pakistani tribal raiders attacked the state. As General Secretary of the National Conference, he:

  • Helped mobilize public opinion in favor of joining India
  • Organized resistance against the Pakistani raiders
  • Worked closely with Sheikh Abdullah and Indian leaders during the crisis
  • Countered Pakistani propaganda that tried to portray the conflict as religious
  • Played a key role in the historic meeting of the Jammu & Kashmir National Conference that endorsed the accession to India
  • Helped maintain communal harmony during this turbulent period
"Kashmir's destiny is intertwined with India's secular democracy. We must resist all forces that try to divide us on religious lines."

Legacy

Political Legacy

Masoodi's steadfast commitment to secularism and democracy in Jammu and Kashmir set an example for future generations. His role in Kashmir's accession to India remains a landmark in the state's history.

Literary Contributions

As an Islamic scholar, Masoodi wrote extensively on Islam's compatibility with democracy and secularism. His works continue to be referenced in discussions about Islam in modern India.

Communal Harmony

Masoodi worked tirelessly to maintain Hindu-Muslim unity in Kashmir during partition. His efforts helped prevent large-scale communal violence in the valley during this turbulent period.

Memorials

Several institutions and places in Jammu and Kashmir are named after Masoodi, including the Maulana Masoodi Memorial Hall in Srinagar. The National Conference commemorates his death anniversary annually.
