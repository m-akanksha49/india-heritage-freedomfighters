Rao Tula Ram

Hero of the First War of Independence (1857), Rao Tula Ram led the rebellion in Haryana and challenged British authority with unmatched courage.

Born

December 9, 1825

Rampura, Rewari, Haryana

Died

September 23, 1863 (aged 37)

Kabul, Afghanistan

Role

Leader of the 1857 revolt in Haryana region

Known For

Militant resistance against British East India Company

"We shall rise with the soil, and our resistance shall echo in every village."

Biography

Rao Tula Ram was born into a royal Yadav family in Rewari, now part of modern-day Haryana. He emerged as one of the most courageous leaders during the Revolt of 1857. A born warrior and administrator, he mobilized local forces and resources to challenge British dominance in the region.

He joined hands with other rebel leaders and even sought foreign assistance from Iran, Afghanistan, and Russia to free India from British rule.

Key Contributions

1857

Led the rebellion in the Rewari region, defeating British forces and taking control of the area temporarily.

1858

Fled to Afghanistan after defeat and began diplomatic efforts to rally support against the British.

1863

Died in Kabul while planning a return campaign with foreign support against the British Raj.

Legacy

Statues & Memorials

Memorials and institutions in Haryana are named after him, especially in Rewari and Delhi.

Education

Rao Tula Ram College of Engineering and other institutions honor his contribution to India’s freedom.

Hero of Rebellion

He remains a symbol of defiance against colonial oppression in North India.

International Appeal

One of the few 1857 leaders who sought global alliances to continue the fight for India’s independence.
