Biography

Born in 1815 in Jowai (present-day Meghalaya), U Kiang Nangbah was a farmer who became the leader of the Jaintia rebellion against British colonial rule. The British had annexed the Jaintia Kingdom in 1835 and imposed heavy taxes while interfering with traditional customs.

In 1860, Nangbah organized a rebellion against British oppression, particularly targeting their salt tax and ban on traditional religious practices. Using guerrilla warfare tactics, his forces attacked British outposts across the Jaintia Hills. The rebellion lasted two years before being suppressed by superior British firepower.

Captured in 1862, Nangbah was publicly hanged in Jowai on December 30. According to legend, he predicted that his face would show no fear at death - and when the execution failed twice (the rope broke twice), he calmly tied the noose himself the third time. His martyrdom inspired future generations of freedom fighters in Northeast India.

Cultural Legacy

U Kiang Nangbah lives on in Meghalaya's cultural memory through:

  • Folk Songs: Jaintia ballads celebrating his bravery
  • Oral Traditions: Stories passed down generations
  • Annual Observances: Martyrdom Day commemorations
  • Dramas: Folk theater performances about his life
  • Art: Paintings and sculptures honoring him
