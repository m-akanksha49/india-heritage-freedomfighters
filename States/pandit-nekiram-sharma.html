Pandit Nekiram Sharma

A bold voice from Haryana in the Indian National Movement, Nekiram Sharma mobilized masses during the Civil Disobedience Movement and was a fierce critic of British rule.

Born

1887

Hansi, Hisar District, Haryana

Died

1956

India

Movements

Civil Disobedience, Quit India, Non-Cooperation

Known For

Mass mobilization in Haryana, journalist, nationalist thinker

"Freedom is earned not by silence, but by fearless voices that awaken the nation."

Biography

Pandit Nekiram Sharma was a leading figure of the freedom struggle in Haryana. Born in 1887 in Hansi, he actively participated in the Indian National Congress and became a close associate of Lala Lajpat Rai. He inspired thousands through his speeches, writings, and organizational ability.

He was repeatedly jailed by the British authorities and was known for promoting Swadeshi and spinning khadi. His work influenced the rural masses of Haryana to rise against colonialism.

Contributions

1921

Led the Non-Cooperation Movement across Hisar and surrounding regions.

1930

Played a vital role in the Civil Disobedience Movement by organizing protests and rallies.

1942

Was imprisoned during the Quit India Movement and tortured by British officials for inciting mass resistance.

Legacy

Journalism

Started nationalist newspapers to awaken political consciousness in Haryana.

Political Organizer

Established local Congress committees and inspired youth in the freedom struggle.

Education Promoter

Encouraged education for rural youth and backward castes in Haryana.

Philosopher

Promoted self-reliance and Indian values through his writings and speeches.
