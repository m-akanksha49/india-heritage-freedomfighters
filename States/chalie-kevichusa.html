Biography

Born in 1923 in Kohima, Chalie Kevichüsa was among the first generation of Western-educated Naga intellectuals. After studying in Shillong and Calcutta, he became deeply involved in the Naga nationalist movement that emerged after India's independence.

Kevichüsa played a pivotal role as a moderate voice in the complex Naga independence struggle. He served as the General Secretary of the Naga National Council (NNC) during critical phases of negotiation with the Indian government. Unlike militant factions, he advocated for political solutions through dialogue.

After the Shillong Accord of 1975, Kevichüsa continued working for Naga rights through constitutional means. His later years were devoted to documenting Naga history and culture. He passed away in 1990, leaving behind a legacy as one of Nagaland's most respected intellectuals and political figures.
