Biography

Born on October 10, 1904 in Pakyong, East Sikkim, Kazi Lhendup Dorji was educated in Darjeeling and Kalimpong. He began his political career in the 1940s, advocating for democratic reforms in the then-independent Himalayan kingdom of Sikkim, which was ruled by a feudal monarchy.

In 1947, he founded the Sikkim State Congress, demanding popular representation and land reforms. After decades of political struggle against the Chogyal (king), Dorji led the historic 1975 movement that resulted in Sikkim's merger with India through a referendum where 97.5% voted in favor. This made Sikkim India's 22nd state on May 16, 1975.

Dorji became Sikkim's first Chief Minister under the new democratic setup, serving from 1975 to 1979. His centenarian life spanned Sikkim's transformation from feudal kingdom to democratic state, and he remained a revered figure until his death at 102 in 2007.

Political Philosophy

Kazi Lhendup Dorji's approach combined traditional Sikkimese values with modern democracy:

  • Inclusive Governance: Empowered all ethnic communities
  • Grassroots Development: Focused on village-level progress
  • Cultural Integrity: Preserved Sikkimese identity within India
  • Non-Aligned Foreign Policy: Maintained Sikkim's strategic balance
