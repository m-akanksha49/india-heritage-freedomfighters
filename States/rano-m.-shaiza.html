Biography

Rano M. Shaiza was born in 1922 in Ukhrul district of Manipur into the Tangkhul Naga community. Breaking barriers from a young age, she became one of the most educated Naga women of her generation. After completing her education, she entered public life at a time when Naga society was predominantly patriarchal.

Shaiza rose to prominence during the turbulent period of Naga nationalist movement in the 1950s-60s. She advocated for peaceful resolution of conflicts and worked to bring the Naga cause to national attention through democratic means. In 1977, she created history by becoming the first woman from Northeast India to be elected to the Lok Sabha, representing Outer Manipur constituency.

Throughout her political career, Shaiza balanced her Naga identity with her commitment to Indian democracy. She fought for tribal rights while also working to integrate Northeast communities into the national mainstream. Her later years were dedicated to women's empowerment and preserving Naga cultural heritage until her passing in 2002.
