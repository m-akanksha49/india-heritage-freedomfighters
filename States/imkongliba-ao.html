Biography

Imkongliba Ao was born in 1910 in Longjang village of Mokokchung district in Nagaland. Educated in missionary schools, he became one of the first educated Naga leaders who could articulate Naga aspirations in the political arena.

He emerged as a prominent leader during the transitional period from British rule to Indian independence. In 1946, he became the first President of the newly formed Naga National Council (NNC), which initially sought to protect Naga interests within the Indian union.

Imkongliba Ao led the Naga delegation to Delhi in 1947 to negotiate with Indian leaders about Naga political future. Though advocating for Naga self-determination, he preferred peaceful dialogue over armed conflict. His sudden death in 1961 marked the end of an important chapter in Naga political history.
