Moje Riba

Moje Riba was the first person from Arunachal Pradesh to be officially recognized as a freedom fighter. He played a key role in spreading nationalist ideas in a region that was remote and disconnected from the heart of India's political movements. His participation in the Quit India Movement made him a symbol of courage and patriotism in the Northeast.

Biography

Moje Riba was born in 1911 in the remote village of Daring, in the present-day West Siang district of Arunachal Pradesh. Growing up during the colonial period, he witnessed the exclusion of the tribal Northeast from mainstream Indian political consciousness. Despite the lack of education and communication in the region, Riba became aware of India’s struggle for independence.

He was inspired by the ideologies of Mahatma Gandhi and the Indian National Congress. As a young man, he began to engage with Congress leaders and worked to bring political awareness to his people. His efforts were not just symbolic—he actively encouraged villagers to resist British rule and supported underground movements. He helped circulate nationalist literature and participated in political discussions, despite the risk of arrest and surveillance.

In 1942, during the Quit India Movement, Riba’s involvement increased significantly. He secretly organized local resistance, despite British intelligence keeping a close watch on him. His actions eventually led to imprisonment and harsh treatment by colonial forces, but his spirit remained unbroken.

Legacy

Padma Shri

Moje Riba was awarded the Padma Shri, one of India’s highest civilian honors, in recognition of his contribution to India's freedom struggle and efforts to integrate Arunachal Pradesh into the national movement.

Pioneer of Political Awareness

He is regarded as the first person to ignite political consciousness among the tribes of Arunachal Pradesh and is often referred to as the 'pioneer of patriotism' in the region.

Symbol of Resistance

Moje Riba's life is celebrated in Arunachal Pradesh through cultural programs, memorials, and school curriculums to inspire future generations.

Community Leader

Even after independence, he continued to serve his community through social and educational initiatives aimed at upliftment of tribal youth.

Interesting Facts

  • Moje Riba was among the few tribal leaders who supported the Indian National Congress when it was almost unheard of in the Northeast.
  • He was imprisoned multiple times but never gave up on the cause of freedom.
  • His efforts laid the groundwork for greater integration of Arunachal Pradesh into India's democratic structure post-1947.
  • He was involved in the establishment of village schools and awareness programs in his later years.

In Popular Culture

Though not as well-known nationally as other freedom fighters, Moje Riba is featured in regional textbooks and documentaries. Several schools and institutions in Arunachal Pradesh are named after him. On India's Independence Day and Republic Day, his name is often mentioned with reverence in official speeches and programs in the Northeast.
