Biography

Sonam Tshering Lepcha was born in 1911 in Dikchu, Sikkim, into the indigenous Lepcha community. From an early age, he witnessed the encroachment of British colonial policies on the traditional lands and rights of the Lepcha people.

He became actively involved in the freedom struggle during the 1930s, organizing resistance against British interference in Sikkim's affairs. Though Sikkim was a princely state, Lepcha recognized the threat of British colonialism to the region's autonomy and indigenous cultures.

After India's independence, he focused on preserving Lepcha language and culture, establishing schools and cultural organizations. He worked tirelessly for the recognition of tribal rights until his death in 1985, leaving behind a legacy of cultural preservation and indigenous activism.
