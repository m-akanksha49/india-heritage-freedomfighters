Bir Bikram Kishore Manikya

The last ruling Maharaja of Tripura (1923–1947), he guided Tripura toward democratic and social reform, establishing schools, hospitals, and laying the foundation for the state's integration with India.

Biography

Born into the Tripuri royal family in 1908, Bir Bikram Kishore Manikya assumed the throne when he was just 15. Influenced by education and progressive governance, he initiated land reforms, launched educational institutions across rural Tripura, and built healthcare facilities, including the GB Hospital in Agartala.

He proclaimed democratic ideas and prepared for Tripura’s integration into India, though he passed away just before Partition. His efforts helped smooth Tripura's merger with India in 1949.

Reforms & Vision

  • Introduced compulsory education and opened rural schools.
  • Established hospitals and health centers for all classes.
  • Implemented land reforms affecting both peasants and tribal farmers.
  • Advocated democracy and equality, paving the way for Tripura's accession to India.
