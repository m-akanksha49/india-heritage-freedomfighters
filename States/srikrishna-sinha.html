Sri Krishna Sinha

First Chief Minister of Bihar and Eminent Freedom Fighter

Sri Krishna Sinha

Fondly known as Shri Babu, he was one of the architects of modern Bihar and played a pivotal role in India’s independence and democratic governance after freedom.

"Freedom is not worth having if it does not include the freedom to make mistakes."

Biography

Born on October 21, 1887, in Bihar, Sri Krishna Sinha was actively involved in the Indian independence movement. He participated in major movements like Non-Cooperation, Civil Disobedience, and Quit India. He was imprisoned several times. After independence, he became the first Chief Minister of Bihar and held the position till 1961, laying the foundation of a democratic, progressive state.

Timeline

1887: Born in Maur, Bihar.
1920–1942: Imprisoned multiple times during India's freedom struggle.
1946: Became the first Premier of Bihar Province.
1950: Became the first Chief Minister of Bihar after Republic formation.
1961: Passed away in office, after 15 years of service.

Legacy

  • Known as the 'Architect of Modern Bihar'.
  • Close associate of Mahatma Gandhi and follower of Gandhian principles.
  • Honored with statues, streets, and institutions in his memory.
