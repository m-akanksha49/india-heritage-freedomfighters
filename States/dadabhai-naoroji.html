Dadabhai Naoroji

Known as the "Grand Old Man of India", Dadabhai Naoroji was a pioneer of Indian nationalism and the first Indian to become a British MP.

Born

September 4, 1825

Navsari, Gujarat, British India

Died

June 30, 1917

Bombay, British India

Title

Grand Old Man of India

Known For

Drain Theory, Indian National Congress Founder, British MP

"Self-government is our birthright, and we must have it."

Biography

Dadabhai Naoroji was one of the earliest leaders to demand self-governance for India. He studied in Bombay and later became a professor and businessman. He was deeply disturbed by British economic policies and worked to expose their exploitative nature.

Naoroji was a founding member of the Indian National Congress and was elected as the first Indian member of the British Parliament in 1892. He is most famous for his Drain Theory, which highlighted how British rule impoverished India by transferring wealth to Britain.
