1931

Execution of Bhagat Singh

On 23 March 1931, Bhagat Singh, Rajguru, and Sukhdev were executed by the British, becoming martyrs and symbols of courage in India's freedom struggle.

Execution of Bhagat Singh, Rajguru, and Sukhdev

Bhagat Singh, Rajguru, and Sukhdev were hanged in Lahore Central Jail for their revolutionary activities against British rule. Their execution sparked nationwide outrage and inspired generations of Indians to fight for freedom.

Why were they executed?

The trio was convicted for their involvement in the killing of British police officer J.P. Saunders and for their revolutionary activities, including the bombing of the Central Legislative Assembly.
