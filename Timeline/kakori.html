The Kakori Train Robbery of 1925 was a daring act by Indian revolutionaries to fund the freedom struggle and challenge British authority.

The Kakori Train Robbery

On August 9, 1925, members of the Hindustan Republican Association (HRA) stopped a train near Kakori, Uttar Pradesh, and looted government funds to finance revolutionary activities. The act was a bold challenge to British rule and inspired a new wave of revolutionary fervor.

Why did it happen?

The robbery was planned to obtain funds for the freedom movement and to send a message of defiance to the British authorities.
