Cabinet Mission Plan

The Cabinet Mission Plan of 1946 was a British initiative to discuss and plan for the transfer of power to Indian leadership and the creation of a new government.

Cabinet Mission Plan

The Cabinet Mission, comprising three British ministers, arrived in India to negotiate with Indian leaders and propose a framework for independence, including a united India with a federal structure.

Why was it important?

The plan aimed to resolve differences between the Congress and the Muslim League, and to avoid partition by proposing a federal union.

Deep Dive: Cabinet Mission Plan

The plan was a last attempt to keep India united, but differences between the Congress and the Muslim League led to its failure and the eventual partition.

Negotiations

Talks between the British, Congress, and Muslim League leaders.

Federal Proposal

The plan proposed a federal union with autonomy for provinces and groups.

Impact

The plan's failure led to the decision to partition India and Pakistan.

Key Figures

Lord Pethick-Lawrence, Sir Stafford Cripps, A.V. Alexander, Jawaharlal Nehru, Muhammad Ali Jinnah.

Legacy

The plan paved the way for the transfer of power and the creation of two nations.

Legacy of the Cabinet Mission Plan

The plan is remembered as the final British effort to keep India united before independence.
