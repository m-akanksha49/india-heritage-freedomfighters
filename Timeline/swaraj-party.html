1923

Swaraj Party Formed

The Swaraj Party was formed in 1923 by Motilal Nehru and Chittaranjan Das to enter legislative councils and obstruct colonial rule from within.

The Formation of the Swaraj Party

After the suspension of the Non-Cooperation Movement, leaders like Motilal Nehru and Chittaranjan Das formed the Swaraj Party to contest elections and voice nationalist demands within the legislative councils.

Why was it formed?

The party was formed to continue the struggle for self-government by legislative means, after the withdrawal of mass movements.

Deep Dive: Swaraj Party

The Swaraj Party played a crucial role in keeping the spirit of nationalism alive during a lull in mass movements, using legislative means to challenge colonial rule.

Legislative Obstruction

The party used its presence in councils to block colonial policies and demand reforms.

Key Leaders

Motilal Nehru, Chittaranjan Das, Vithalbhai Patel, and others.

Simon Commission Boycott

The party joined the national boycott of the Simon Commission in 1928.

Impact on Freedom Movement

The party kept nationalist issues in the public eye and prepared the ground for future mass movements.

Legacy

The Swaraj Party is remembered for its role in parliamentary opposition to colonial rule.

Legacy of the Swaraj Party

The party demonstrated the value of legislative action and unity in the struggle for self-government.
