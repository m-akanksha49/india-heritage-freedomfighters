Mountbatten Plan

The Mountbatten Plan of 1947 outlined the process for the partition of British India and the creation of the independent nations of India and Pakistan.

Mountbatten Plan

Proposed by Lord Louis Mountbatten, the last Viceroy of India, the plan called for the partition of India along religious lines, leading to the creation of India and Pakistan on 15 August 1947.

Why was it important?

The plan ended British rule in India, but also resulted in the largest mass migration and communal violence in history.

Historical Timeline

Deep Dive: Mountbatten Plan

The plan was a response to growing communal tensions and political deadlock, resulting in the division of the subcontinent and the end of colonial rule.

Partition

The plan divided British India into two dominions: India and Pakistan.

Mass Migration

Millions migrated across the new borders, facing violence and hardship.

Impact

The plan ended British rule but left a legacy of division and conflict.

Key Figures

Legacy

The plan is remembered for both independence and the tragedy of partition.
