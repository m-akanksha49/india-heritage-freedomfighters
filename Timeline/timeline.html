⚔️ Phase 1: Early Resistance (1757–1857)
1757
Battle that started British dominance

Battle of Plassey

Robert Clive's victory over Siraj-ud-Daulah established British East India Company's dominance in Bengal, marking the beginning of British colonial rule in India.

1764
Decisive British victory in Bengal

Battle of Buxar

British victory over combined forces of Mir Qasim, Shuja-ud-Daula, and Shah Alam II strengthened their control over Bengal and Bihar, establishing them as the dominant power in northern India.

Explore
1799
Fall of Mysore's Tiger

Death of Tipu Sultan

The Tiger of Mysore fell during the 4th Anglo-Mysore War, ending significant resistance in South India against British expansion.

Explore
1806
First sepoy uprising

Vellore Mutiny

First large-scale sepoy uprising against British dress regulations and religious interference, foreshadowing the 1857 rebellion.

Explore
1831
Coorg uprising against British

Rebellion of Kodagu

Uprising in Coorg against British annexation led by Virarajendra, demonstrating early resistance to colonial expansion in South India.

Explore
1855
Tribal uprising in Jharkhand

Santhal Rebellion

Tribal uprising in Jharkhand against British and zamindari oppression led by Sidhu and Kanhu, one of the largest peasant revolts.

Explore
1857
First major war for independence

First War of Independence

Massive uprising led by Mangal Pandey, Rani Lakshmi Bai, Tantia Tope, and Bahadur Shah Zafar against British rule, marking a turning point in Indian history.

1858
End of Company rule

British Crown Rule Begins

End of East India Company rule after the 1857 revolt, beginning of direct British Crown governance through the Government of India Act 1858.

Explore
1875
Hindu reform movement

Arya Samaj Founded

Swami Dayanand Saraswati establishes reformist Hindu organization promoting education, nationalism, and social reform.

Explore
1876
First nationalist organization

Indian National Association

First nationalist organization founded by Surendranath Banerjee and Anand Mohan Bose, precursor to the Indian National Congress.

Explore
1885
Birth of INC

Indian National Congress Formed

A.O. Hume helps establish INC, which would lead the freedom struggle for decades, becoming the principal voice of Indian nationalism.

Explore
1893
Cultural nationalism

Tilak's Cultural Nationalism

Bal Gangadhar Tilak promotes Ganesh Utsav and Shivaji Jayanti to foster national unity and resistance against British rule.

Explore
1897
First revolutionary act

Chapekar Brothers' Assassination

Damodar and Balkrishna Chapekar assassinate British officer Rand in Pune, marking early revolutionary activity against British rule.

Explore
1904
Revolutionary organization

Anushilan Samiti Formed

Revolutionary nationalist organization established in Bengal by Pramathanath Mitra, advocating armed resistance against British rule.

🔥 Phase 3: Partition & Swadeshi Movement (1905–1917)
1905
Divide and rule policy

Partition of Bengal

Lord Curzon divides Bengal on religious lines, sparking massive protests and the Swadeshi movement, galvanizing nationalist sentiment.

Explore
1905–1908
Economic nationalism

Swadeshi & Boycott Movements

Massive nationalist campaigns promoting Indian goods and boycotting British products, marking the beginning of economic nationalism.

Explore
1906
Muslim political organization

Muslim League Formed

Political organization established to represent Muslim interests, later demanding Pakistan under Muhammad Ali Jinnah's leadership.

Explore
1907
Congress divides

Surat Split

INC divides between Moderates (Gokhale) favoring constitutional methods and Extremists (Tilak, Lajpat Rai, Pal) advocating stronger resistance.

Explore
1909
Separate electorates introduced

Morley-Minto Reforms

Introduced separate electorates for Muslims, sowing seeds of communalism while expanding Indian participation in governance.

Explore
1911
Nationalist victory

Partition of Bengal Annulled

1915
Mahatma returns to India

Gandhi Returns to India

After developing satyagraha in South Africa, Gandhi joins Indian freedom struggle with nonviolent resistance methods.

Explore
1917
First satyagraha in India

Champaran Satyagraha

Gandhi's first satyagraha in India supporting indigo farmers against exploitative British planters, establishing his leadership.

Explore
1918
Peasant movement in Gujarat

Kheda Satyagraha

Gandhi supports peasants in Gujarat affected by famine and high taxes, demonstrating mass mobilization potential.

Explore
1919
British massacre in Amritsar

Jallianwala Bagh Massacre

General Dyer orders troops to fire on peaceful gathering in Amritsar, killing hundreds and galvanizing nationalist movement.

Explore
1919
Limited self-government

Montagu-Chelmsford Reforms

Introduces dyarchy system but fails to satisfy nationalist demands for self-rule, leading to Non-Cooperation Movement.

Explore
1920
Mass civil disobedience

Non-Cooperation Movement

Gandhi launches mass movement boycotting British institutions, titles, and goods, marking shift to mass politics.

Explore
1922
Violence ends movement

Chauri Chaura Incident

Violent clash between police and protesters leads Gandhi to call off Non-Cooperation Movement, emphasizing nonviolence.

Explore
1923
Congress faction formed

Swaraj Party Formed

C.R. Das and Motilal Nehru form party to contest elections and obstruct British rule from within legislative councils.

Explore
1925
Revolutionary action

Kakori Train Robbery

Revolutionaries including Ram Prasad Bismil loot British government money train to fund independence activities.

Explore
1927
All-white commission

Simon Commission Arrives

All-white British commission on constitutional reforms met with nationwide protests and "Go Back Simon" slogans.

Explore
1928
Indian constitutional proposal

Nehru Report

Motilal Nehru chairs committee proposing dominion status constitution for India, rejected by more radical nationalists.

Explore
1929
Demand for complete independence

Purna Swaraj Declared

Lahore Congress session under Jawaharlal Nehru demands complete independence, setting stage for Civil Disobedience Movement.

🧂 Phase 5: Civil Disobedience & Rise of Revolutionaries (1930–1939)
1930
Salt Satyagraha

Dandi March

Gandhi's 240-mile Salt Satyagraha challenging British salt tax laws, a defining moment in Civil Disobedience Movement.

Explore
1931
Truce agreement

Gandhi-Irwin Pact

Truce agreement between Gandhi and Viceroy Irwin ending Civil Disobedience Movement and securing Congress participation in Round Table Conference.

Explore
1931
Martyrs of freedom struggle

Execution of Bhagat Singh

Revolutionaries Bhagat Singh, Rajguru, and Sukhdev hanged for Lahore conspiracy case, becoming icons of resistance.

Explore
1932
Dalit representation

Poona Pact

Agreement between Gandhi and Ambedkar on political representation for Dalits, avoiding separate electorates.

Explore
1935
Constitutional reforms

Government of India Act

Provides provincial autonomy but retains British control at center, becoming basis for independent India's constitution.

Explore
1939
Radical faction

Bose Forms Forward Bloc

Subhas Chandra Bose resigns from INC after ideological differences with Gandhi and forms new radical faction advocating direct action.

Explore

Explore

Massive revolt by Indian sailors in Bombay and Karachi against British officers, showing widespread discontent with colonial rule.

Explore
1946
Constitutional proposal

Cabinet Mission Plan

British proposal for united India with limited autonomy, ultimately rejected leading to partition.

Explore
1947
Partition plan

Mountbatten Plan

Final partition plan dividing India and Pakistan, setting August 15 independence date amid communal violence.

Explore
1947
India becomes free

India Gains Independence

At midnight on August 15, India becomes a free nation after 200 years of British rule, with Jawaharlal Nehru as first PM.
