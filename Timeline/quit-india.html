Quit India Movement

The Quit India Movement of 1942 was a mass protest demanding an end to British rule in India, marked by the slogan "Do or Die" and widespread civil disobedience.

Quit India Movement

Launched on 8 August 1942 by Mahatma Gandhi and the Indian National Congress, the movement called for immediate independence and led to mass protests, strikes, and arrests across the country.

Why was it important?

The movement was the final major campaign against British rule, uniting Indians in a common cause and hastening the end of colonialism.
