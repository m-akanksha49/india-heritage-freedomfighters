Dandi March

The Dandi March, led by Mahatma Gandhi in 1930, was a pivotal act of civil disobedience against the British salt monopoly, sparking the Civil Disobedience Movement.

The Dandi March

On 12 March 1930, Mahatma Gandhi and 78 followers set out from Sabarmati Ashram to Dandi, Gujarat, covering 240 miles in 24 days to break the salt law. The march galvanized the nation and marked a new phase in the freedom struggle.

Why was it important?

The march was a nonviolent protest against the British salt tax, inspiring mass participation and international attention.
