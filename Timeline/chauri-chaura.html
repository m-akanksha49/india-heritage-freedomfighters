The Chauri Chaura Incident

On February 4, 1922, a protest in Chauri Chaura, Uttar Pradesh, turned violent when demonstrators clashed with police and set fire to a police station, resulting in the deaths of 22 policemen. The incident shocked Gandhi, who immediately called off the Non-Cooperation Movement, emphasizing the importance of nonviolence.

Why did it happen?

The incident was triggered by police brutality against peaceful protesters, leading to a violent backlash. Gandhi's decision to suspend the movement demonstrated his commitment to nonviolence.
