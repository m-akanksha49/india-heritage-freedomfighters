1928

Simon Commission Arrives

The arrival of the Simon Commission in 1928 was met with nationwide protests, as it included no Indian members and symbolized colonial disregard for Indian opinion.

The Simon Commission

The Simon Commission, appointed by the British government to review constitutional reforms, arrived in India in 1928. Its all-British composition led to widespread boycotts and protests, uniting Indians across political lines.

Why was it opposed?

The commission had no Indian members, which was seen as an insult to Indian self-respect and a denial of the right to self-determination.
