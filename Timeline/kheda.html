Kheda Satyagraha

The Kheda Satyagraha was launched in 1918 in Gujarat, where farmers were unable to pay taxes due to crop failure and famine. Gandhi led the protest, demanding the suspension of tax collection.

Why Kheda?

Despite crop failure, the British government insisted on collecting taxes. Gandhi's intervention united the farmers and forced the government to grant relief.

Deep Dive: Kheda Satyagraha

The Kheda Satyagraha was a major milestone in India's freedom movement. It demonstrated the effectiveness of collective action and nonviolent protest.

Famine and Hardship

Farmers in Kheda faced severe famine and were unable to pay taxes.

Gandhi and Patel's Leadership

Gandhi and Sardar Patel united the farmers and led the protest.

Victory for Farmers

The government agreed to suspend tax collection, marking a major victory.

Impact on Freedom Movement

The success of Kheda inspired future satyagrahas and established Gandhi as a national leader.

Legacy

The Kheda Satyagraha is remembered as a model of nonviolent protest and collective action.

Legacy of Kheda Satyagraha

The movement proved the power of unity and nonviolent protest in achieving justice.
