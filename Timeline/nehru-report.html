1928

Nehru Report

The Nehru Report of 1928 was the first major attempt by Indians to draft a constitution for self-government, demanding dominion status within the British Empire.

The Nehru Report

Drafted by a committee headed by Motilal Nehru, the Nehru Report was a response to the Simon Commission and outlined a vision for a democratic, secular, and federal India with dominion status.

Why was it important?

The report was the first Indian effort to frame a constitution, reflecting the aspirations of the nationalist movement and setting the stage for future demands for complete independence.

Deep Dive: Nehru Report

The Nehru Report was a milestone in the constitutional history of India, reflecting the growing unity and political maturity of the nationalist movement.

Dominion Status

The report demanded dominion status for India within the British Empire.

Fundamental Rights

The report outlined fundamental rights and a secular, federal structure for India.

Rejection and Aftermath

The British rejection of the report led to the demand for complete independence (Purna Swaraj).

Key Figures

Motilal Nehru, Jawaharlal Nehru, Tej Bahadur Sapru, Subhas Chandra Bose.

Legacy

The report set the stage for the demand for Purna Swaraj and the framing of the Indian Constitution.

Legacy of the Nehru Report

The Nehru Report is remembered as a bold assertion of Indian aspirations and a key step towards independence.
